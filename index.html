01 Live · In Production

Market Intelligence Platform

The biggest project — an end-to-end AI-powered market intelligence pipeline built entirely from scratch. Microsoft Graph API ingests broker research emails from Outlook, Azure Blob stores raw data, GPT-4.1-mini classifies by asset class and extracts investment themes, Azure SQL stores everything, and a Flask dashboard with full-text search and date filtering serves the investment team. Azure Functions trigger the pipeline every 15 minutes automatically. Includes an AI chatbot powered by Claude that lets users ask questions directly about the ingested research emails and market data.

04 Built

AI Trade Council

Multi-agent system where AI agents collaborate on live market analysis. Each asset gets a full signal confluence report — RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, EMA crossovers, candlestick patterns — then Claude and Perplexity cross-check the thesis. Outputs BUY / SELL / HOLD with entry price, target, stop-loss, risk/reward ratio, and a discipline check. Final signals are pushed directly to a Telegram bot.

About Me

Jade Tan Hong Yu

First-year Economics & Finance student

I'm Jade. I started out studying coding at NYP, but somewhere along the way I realised I was just as interested in markets and investing as I was in building things. Instead of choosing one, I decided to pursue both. Today, I'm studying Economics & Finance at the University of London while continuing to build technology solutions for finance teams.

Most recently at Raintree Asset Management, I built an AI-powered market intelligence platform that pulls research from multiple banks, processes it through Azure and AI workflows, and turns it into a daily digest for the CIO team. Seeing something I built become part of an actual investment process was what made me realise how much I enjoy working at the intersection of finance and technology.

Outside of work, I'm a Senior Analyst with the Student Investment Club, where I conduct equity research and develop investment theses. I'm also Vice President and Co-Head of Training for the Business Strategy Club, where I teach consulting frameworks, case-solving techniques and structured problem-solving. I recently competed in Think Cell's case competition, working on strategic recommendations for Netflix.

Beyond finance and technology, I'm passionate about giving back to the community. Over the years, I've volunteered with organisations such as Willing Hearts, Causes for Animals, St. Luke's ElderCare and tutoring programmes for underprivileged students. These experiences have taught me empathy, patience and the importance of creating meaningful impact beyond the workplace.

I'm naturally curious and enjoy figuring out how things work — whether that's analysing a company, building a data pipeline, or solving an operational problem. I like turning complexity into something useful.

Long term, I'm looking for opportunities where finance, strategy and technology come together. The most exciting problems, in my view, sit at the intersection of all three.
